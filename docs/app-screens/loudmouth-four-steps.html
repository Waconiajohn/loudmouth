Step One
Listen
Really hear it. Before you sing a single note.
🎙️
Most singers skip this step entirely. Don't. The professionals who make it look effortless spent hours just listening. They know where every breath happens, where the artist's voice cracks, what makes the chorus explode. You're about to know your song like that.
Listen through once — just enjoy it
No analyzing. No singing along. Just let it wash over you. Remind yourself why you love this song.
Find the hardest moment
Every song has one phrase that trips people up. Where is it? The big note, the key change, the fast verse. Name it. That's your target.
Notice where the artist breathes
Listen for the inhales between phrases. That's your roadmap. Breathing where the artist breathes is the single fastest way to sound like you belong in the song.
Read the lyrics once through
Out loud. Slowly. What is this song actually about? When you know the meaning, the emotion comes naturally. Judges don't score notes — they score feeling.
2
Step Two · Your Daily Music Meditation
Breathe
Prepare the instrument. The lungs are the engine.
🧘
This is Your Daily Music Meditation. What you're about to do is what every professional does before they open their mouth — and almost no amateur does. Five minutes here will change what happens in five minutes when you sing. The voice you have right after breathwork is not the same voice you had before it.
2 min — Box Breathing
4 counts in. 4 hold. 4 out. 4 hold. Four rounds. This resets your nervous system. If you have stage fright, this is the cure that works in under two minutes.
2 min — Diaphragmatic Breathing
One hand on your chest. One on your belly. Only the belly moves. If your chest is rising, start over. This is how you access the engine.
1 min — Humming warmup
Sustained "Mmm" hum. Feel it in your chest, not your throat. Sixty seconds of this is worth more than twenty minutes of scales. Your folds are now warm. You're ready.
3
Step Three
Blend
Sing with the artist. Borrow their voice tonight.
🎚️
The Vocal Assist Slider is all the way to the right. The artist's real vocal is blended behind yours — not a cover version, the actual recording. Right now you're not trying to be a hero. You're learning the song from the inside. Let the artist carry you through it.
Your Slider — Start Here
← All You Full Assist →
Slider at 75% assist for your first pass. You'll hear yourself singing. The artist is the safety net underneath you.
First sing-through — high assist
Slider at 75%. Sing the whole song. Don't stop, don't correct yourself mid-phrase. Just go. The goal is to finish the song, not to be perfect.
Drop to 50% — sing again
Now you're in it. 50/50 — you and the artist, equal partners. Notice what gets harder. That's useful information, not failure.
Drill the hard moment
Remember the hardest part from Step 1? Loop just that section. Repeat it five times. Let the assist help you through it until you know the shape of it.
4
Step Four
Own It
Pull the slider left. This is your voice now.
🏆
The scaffold comes down. The artist's vocal is barely a whisper now. What you're hearing is almost entirely you. This is what the training has been building toward — a voice that knows the song from the inside, with the strength to deliver it. This is your Loudmouth.
Your Slider — Pull It Left
← All You Full Assist →
Slider at 20% or less. That whisper of the artist is just a reference, not a crutch. You've got this.
The performance run
Slider at 20% or below. Sing the whole song like there's a crowd. Because there will be. This is the take. Don't stop for anything.
Review your score
Four dimensions: Pitch, Timing, Clarity, Dynamics. Look at which dimension is lowest. That's your one thing to work on. Not five things. One.
Beat the artist
The artist's score is right there. Beat it. It's their song — and you're singing it better than they do at their own casual bar performance level. That's the point. You own it now.
