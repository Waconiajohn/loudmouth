How the Timing Guide Works
Word dots — each upcoming word appears on the runway ahead of the now-line. They flow left toward you like a conveyor belt.
Amber glow — the next word brightens as it approaches the now-line. This is your anticipation cue — your cue to open your mouth.
Green flash — you entered right on time. You'll barely notice it when you're in the zone.
Amber flash + "A touch early" — you came in slightly before the beat. Common on choruses you know well.
Blue flash + "Just behind" — you came in slightly late. Usually means you hesitated. The runway gives you the visual countdown to fix it.
Breath markers — vertical tick marks show where the phrase ends and you should breathe. They match where the artist breathes.
